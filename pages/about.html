About

I am Matthew Satterfield.

Currently I am studying Game Development at NEIT.

A few of my hobbies include: composing music, playing piano, playing video games, misc. programming projects, and participating in game jams. I get a lot of satisfaction out of problem solving, and often theorizing a solution or idea can be more fun than actually implementing it for me.

I try to prioritize quality over quantity; a game with only one simple yet polished mechanic is often better than a game with multiple complex unpolished mechanics, in my opinion. This prioritization also is benefitial when dealing with scope, as it helps me keep a bit of a smaller more achievable scope.
